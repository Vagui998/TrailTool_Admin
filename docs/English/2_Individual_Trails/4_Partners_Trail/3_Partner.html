Why Engage in Transdisciplinary Education?

The society changes fast, and not always towards the better. So-called ‘wicked problems’ are accountable for important parts of this change. These new emerging challenges call for a broader approach, bridging many disciplines and societal actors to collectively co-create sustainable futures. To safeguard our own and societies future, we should question and adapt our working methods. Transdisciplinarity is a building block in these changes. Participating in transdisciplinary education specifically, provides the possibility to develop new perspectives on existing challenges, and prepares a new generation of professionals to approach the future in a way that meets the requirements of an ever-changing world.

Advancing future skill sets

Prospective professionals need problem-solving skills, such as collaborating with - and understanding of - people with different points of view, the ability to perceive how different viewpoints are interconnected within a larger system, an open attitude and willingness to learn from others, valuing diverse forms of knowledge. Education needs to train people who can make changes and face recent problems effectively. It gives space to personal development and general competences to obtain these skills while working in real-life world problems. By bringing in the voice and perspectives from everyday life stakeholders, you can contribute to the educational formation this generation. A generation that you are probably awaiting as well.

Wicked problems

Young people should not only be educated to become a skilled professional, but they are also the next generation who must carry the complex societal problems of tomorrow. To train them in early stages to deal with these so-called wicked problems (problems without a clear solution, in which multiple perspectives are needed to address the problem), they need to be challenged with real-life problems. In this way, they learn to understand that wicked problems are beyond disciplines and collaboration is essential. You, as a partner from this ‘real’ world, are essential to create learning communities together with researchers, teachers and students from different disciplines. As we address big challenges in these communities, all parties learn about the wicked problem, can include new perspectives, and contribute to serious change.

Life-long learning

A transdisciplinary education includes learning for everyone involved, not just students. Thus, as a partner, you should approach this TRAIL with the mindset to learn yourself and be willing to address changing factors in your organization or as a citizen.
