What

    Before explaining why transdisciplinarity is important, it is necessary to understand its essential meaning. Despite the existence of multiple understandings, this TRAIL defines transdisciplinarity as “the integration of academic researchers from different disciplines with non-academic participants in the co-creation of new knowledge and theory to achieve a common goal” (OECD, 2021). This definition paves the way for distinguishing three characteristic features.

    The first relates to the type of collaboration partners involved. In transdisciplinarity, partners from academic institutes and especially non-academic backgrounds are involved in the design or research process. Furthermore, this team should exist of researchers/experts of different disciplinary backgrounds. Indeed, the seemingly similar concept of interdisciplinarity is an intrinsic part here, however with the addition of broader societal voices as well. The scope of participant backgrounds ranging behind academia – thus, your participation – is what sets transdisciplinarity collaborations apart.

    This diverse group of transdisciplinary participants is bounded by the issue in which they are involved, the second characteristic feature. Transdisciplinarity is by its very nature about involvement in complex societal issues where there is no clear solution and working method to arrive at that solution. These type problems are commonly referred to as wicked problems (Williams, 2022). Because straightforward definitions and solutions are impossible for these types of problems, transdisciplinarity arrives as a new working method.

    Transdisciplinarity can be understood as a metatheory, a new kind of knowledge. It adopts different methodologies from different disciplines to transform and transcend them. It ensures the emergence of new concepts and approaches related to the wicked problem at hand. As these problems are always grounded in the real world - thanks to the collaboration with you, non-academic partners, transdisciplinarity serves society in a direct way (Mokiy, 2019).

    The third feature of transdiscplinarity distinguished here, relates to the form of involvement. With transdisciplinarity, all partners work together based on equality. There is no single way to define the mutual relations, as everyone should be learning together to generate new knowledge and innovative idea (Visscher, 2023). As one author once said: here are no transdisciplinary experts, only transdisciplinary practitioners (Mokiy, 2019).

    The TRAIL provides extensive guidelines and insights on the practical effects and challenges of these three features. The TRAIL focuses on transdisciplinary education, which means that students are part of the transdisciplinary project. The tool is created with the focus on education. However, ideally, all participants add the same value. In the end, transdisciplinarity is very much about learning and transformation for everyone involved.
