How to get together

Remember the values

Now we stand at the start of the participation phase, we quickly go back to basics one more time. The practice of transdisciplinary education can be ambiguous, filled with practical doubts and struggles. Especially when these problems occur, it is important to remember the core-values. Recall what stayed with you from entering the TRAIL. Transdisciplinary projects are designed to make progress on real-world problems, empower futureproof education, and hear the voices of all actors involved. This requires genuine interests in other participants and equality among team members.

Recruitment

Before starting off, the first step is the recruitment of project participants, as far as the complete team has not been formed in the basecamp or design phase. There is the possibility to recruit based on background (disciplines), motivation, or grades (in case of the students). Motivation is an important criterion because every motivated participant should have the opportunity to participate in transdisciplinary education.

Safe environment

Next, when actually coming together, you really feel that the journey progresses – an exciting reality. Following the transdisciplinary core values, we need to ensure mutual trust and freedom for everyone. Establishing a safe environment is a must for successful transdisciplinary collaboration. In a next page below, lessons from the TRAIL-research and the Praktijkgids Transdiscipinair werken (Het Groene Brein, 2023), help you getting started with this.

Co-creation

After kicking off, you will soon discover the challenges in the practical organization of collaboration. The presence of many different team members also means: many different agendas, meetings, and preferences to take into account. In order to keep track and don’t lose the collaborative flow, a specific page provides you with practical organization advice from experienced experts. For example, have a look at the Learning Community Tool, which provides an insightful and hands-on tool for the development of co-creation. This tool among others suggests sticking to weekly team meetings, creating a blended community according to five design principles, and using concrete already existing collaboration tools, for which this TRAIL provides inspiration as well.

Nobody said it would be easy, but exactly therefore we embark on the most exciting phase of this adventure: lets participate!
