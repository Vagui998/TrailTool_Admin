Roles and tasks

Which mindsets, roles, and tasks does a transdisciplinary team have? And how about you: how exactly can you participate and contribute to the project? We distinguish different tasks and related roles in a transdisciplinary project. These include: acting, decision making, and guiding. Different roles can, of course, and probably will overlap.

Acting

This basic task of ‘acting’ in a process concerns your own contributions, no matter your role in the process or position as a stakeholder. Don’t hesitate to take responsibility, get off your own beaten track but also make sure you ask others to do the same. Remember that you are in it together. Another shared attitude intrinsic to transdisciplinarity is a research mindset. As nothing is clear at the beginning of a transdisciplinary project, you should be ready to create and explore new knowledge.

Decision making

Decisions in the project are made by different actors, not just those with the most experience or the loudest voice. In the safe environment of the transdisciplinary project, everyone should be listened to.

Guiding

At the same time, sometimes one individual has to oversee the entire process, spark break thoughts, and balance the needs of the group with the practical framework. We call these people ‘guides’. Guides have 2 distinct roles in transdisciplinary projects. They first of all set up and co-design the TRAIL, and later on foster the processes as coaches. This applies especially, but not solely, to the educational environment in which teachers or other students can guide the learning process of participating students.

No matter the role you adopt during the project, you also enter the process from a certain stakeholders position. The previous experience from this position will almost certainly influence the way you experience a transdisciplinary process. Therefore, the following sub-pages discuss the roles and tasks for each stakeholder, including particular typical changes in habits compared to disciplinary professional or educational realities.
