No predetermined result

The direction in which the team is exploring solutions is not set in advance. The point of transdisciplinary learning is to be innovative. This comes with the risk that the team may not be advancing too much in the direction of a solution. If they choose to stay longer with the problem, this may also be very valuable. Since there is the possibility of understanding the problem better from different perspectives. Often, solutions to certain problems are not suitable since the problem is being misunderstood. Therefore, understanding the problem or recognizing the complexity of the problem can be a crucial outcome.

Understanding the not-knowing

A possible outcome is the understanding that there is no joint solution for the specific problem. This can be disappointing. However, it can also create the possibility to step back to redefine the problem and to look at the system in which the problem is created. This could lead to further questions or different problems that need to be addressed first to tackle the chosen wicked problem.

Understanding multiple perspectives

Another possible outcome could be the understanding of different perspectives on the same problem. This can be achieved by working with people from another discipline, another sector, and/or another cultural background. In this sense, boundaries are crossed. This also opens a possibility to reflect on your own perspective. Questions arise such as: how do I look at the problem? It helps with defining your own positionality and understanding the complexity of multiple perspectives.

Set expectations

You are now aware that the outcome of a transdisciplinary collaboration is not just the innovative idea or “product” that the team comes up with. To avoid disappointment from the side of the students, other teachers, or stakeholders you need to carefully manage the expectations from the start.
