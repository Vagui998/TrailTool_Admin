Visible outcomes: knowledge acquisition, action-oriented outcomes

Although there is no guarantee for a predetermined result, a transdisciplinary collaboration can lead to a wide variety of project outcomes because it is learner-driven. It might lead to innovative solutions to local challenges such as water usage, or a pitch or a proposition to local officials, or a conference for local stakeholders, or a game, or a movie, or a comic, or a podcast, or a website, … These are all tangible outcomes of the project that could be assessed on their direct impact.

Based on the learning goals and the tool of ‘innovatie werkplaatsen’ (Cremers et. Al, 2017), there are multiple possible tangible outcomes such as knowledge acquisition or action-oriented outcomes.

Knowledge/products

One of the possible outcomes is knowledge acquisition in the form of concepts, instruments, or models to support others to tackle the wicked problem that is worked upon. Furthermore, design products in the form of a prototype can be created.

Action-oriented outcomes

Another form of outcome could be action-oriented. This means that there can be an intervention in a certain place. This could be in the form of a workshop, a meeting, a training, a gathering, or a change in the environment. Therefore, participants need to roll up their sleeves and act upon a transformation in the environment they are working in. An example of such an action-oriented outcome is a workshop in the Act for Change minor at Avans University of Applied Science. As part of this program workshop, students of worked together with refugees to mobilize them to connect to each other through sport activities. It can be a small gesture, that grows into something bigger. This way, fertile ground for societal growth is created by a transdisciplinary project.
