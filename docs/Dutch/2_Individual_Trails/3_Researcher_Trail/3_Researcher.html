Rethinking Academia: Navigating the Future through Transdisciplinary Working

The cracks in societal, ecological, and planetary well-being are calling for a change in research and education. The ‘Wicked problems’ call for a broader approach, bridging many disciplines and societal actors to collectively co-create sustainable futures. Education needs to produce change-makers who can address evolving challenges. New skills and competencies should go beyond traditional boundaries. Knowledge should be produced in ways matching the complexity of the subjects at hand.


To meet the demands of an evolving society, academia must transcend traditional approaches. The future hinges on a population capable of addressing both large and small societal challenges, demanding new research approaches (OECD, 2020) and innovative pedagogies in higher education (Jensen-Pennington, 2021). Researchers play a pivotal role in this transformative journey, shaping the goals, methods, and assessments that are used to understand the world and transform it for a better future.

To enable these transformations, a shift towards transdisciplinary projects is proposed. These concepts offer a platform for researchers to foster meaningful relationships with the studied problems and all its stakeholders, nurture their own competences and that of future generations as well.

The process of transdisciplinary collaboration brings together researchers, policy makers, entrepreneurs, civil society, and students from the very start to map out the complexity of the challenge. There is a clear need for a broader view on the challenges and adequate system-understanding. A rule of thumb for approaching complex problems is to match the complexity of the challenge with the complexity of the team. In this way, you can gather insight into the diversity of dimensions.

To co-create a more sustainable future, new skills and competences are needed. These people need to understand the complexity of the world and the societal challenges and work with the future to bring about change. Education needs to deliver these change-makers. Students, who are already witnessing or living with these wicked problems, wish for their education to prepare them how to address these problems to navigate towards a more sustainable future for themselves and for society. Society relies on the education system to provide students the space and time to develop their talents and cultivate transversal skills they will need in the future. Transdisciplinary education therefore is a central part of the transdisciplinary projects explored in this TRAIL.
